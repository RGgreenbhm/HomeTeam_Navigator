## Understanding Costs

### For Most Medicare Beneficiaries:
APCM is covered by Medicare Part B. You may have a **monthly copay or coinsurance** (typically 20% of the Medicare-approved amount), similar to other Medicare services.

### For Qualified Medicare Beneficiaries (QMB):
If you have QMB status through Medicaid, you have **no out-of-pocket cost** for APCM services.

### Important Note:
Your specific costs depend on your Medicare plan and supplemental coverage. We can help you understand your costs before you enroll.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Is APCM different from my regular visits?
**A:** Yes! APCM provides *ongoing* care coordination throughout the month‚Äînot just during appointments. You'll receive proactive support, regular medication reviews, and coordination with your other healthcare providers.

### Q: Will I still have regular office visits?
**A:** Absolutely. APCM is *in addition to* your regular visits, not a replacement. You'll continue to see your provider as needed.

### Q: Can I use APCM with Medicare Advantage?
**A:** APCM is primarily for Original Medicare (Part B). If you have Medicare Advantage, please ask us about your options.

### Q: What if I already see another doctor for APCM?
**A:** Medicare only allows one provider to bill APCM for you at a time. If you're receiving APCM elsewhere, you would need to stop that service before enrolling with us.

### Q: How do I reach my care team between visits?
